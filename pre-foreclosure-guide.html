Understanding the Foreclosure Timeline in New Jersey

Here's something most people don't know: New Jersey is a judicial foreclosure state. Your lender must go through the court system ‚Äî one of the slowest in the country.

Key fact: The NJ foreclosure process can take 1 to 3+ years from first missed payment to sheriff sale. You have time ‚Äî but you should start now.

Section Three

Your 6 Options

You have more options than you think. Here are six paths forward, ranked from best to most drastic.

Option What It Means Best If‚Ä¶
1. Reinstatement Pay what you're behind (including fees) to bring your mortgage current. You had a temporary hardship and now have the funds.
2. Loan Modification Change your loan terms ‚Äî lower rate, extended period, or reduced principal. Contact your servicer's loss mitigation department. You can afford a lower payment but not the current one.
3. Sell Before Foreclosure ‚òÖ Selling puts cash in your pocket, pays off your mortgage, and protects your credit. Many Bayonne homeowners have significant equity they don't realize. You have equity and want to walk away with money and credit intact.
4. Short Sale Negotiate with the bank to accept less than the full balance. Requires lender approval, but far less damaging than foreclosure. You're "underwater" but want to avoid foreclosure on your record.
5. Deed in Lieu Voluntarily transfer ownership to the lender. Avoids court and may minimize deficiency judgments. Other options exhausted; want to avoid the court process.
6. Bankruptcy (Ch. 13) An automatic stay halts foreclosure immediately. Creates a 3‚Äì5 year repayment plan. Consult a qualified attorney. You need court protection to keep your home. Last resort.
